Unity Projects

Where creativity had no limits and shipping didn't matter yet.

Before the platforms and the mods — there was game dev.

These were never published. That was the point. Unity game prototypes were where I learned what it actually means to build something — physics systems, game mechanics, player interactions, level design, C# architecture. Started at age 10–11 after finishing two Udemy Unity certifications, and the projects kept coming.

No release pressure. No feature requirements. Just ideas and the tools to execute them. I built more games in this era than I can count. Platformers, physics sandboxes, top-down shooters, puzzle games. Most of them unfinished. All of them useful.

I never posted a game from this era — but I built more than I can count. And that's what mattered.
