Where the modding journey started

Before Rift, before modrift, before any of the platform work — there was this. A Minecraft mod, built in Java, using IntelliJ IDEA, published to CurseForge. My first time working with a real professional IDE and a real modding framework.

More Curse Enchantments adds unique gameplay mechanics and curse enchantments to Minecraft. The content mattered less than what building it taught me — how Java really works when you're not in a tutorial, how to navigate a large framework you didn't write, how to debug things when Stack Overflow has no answer for your specific problem.

This mod is the direct predecessor to Rift Loader. I published a mod, learned the problems with existing mod infrastructure, and decided to build better infrastructure. That's how Rift was born.

Getting a mod onto CurseForge, versioning, writing documentation people can actually use.
