The Watcher

It knows your patterns. It learns your habits. It's been watching since day one.

Psychological horror, engineered.

The Watcher is not a jump-scare mod. It doesn't spawn random monsters or throw scripted events at you. It watches. From the moment you first load the world, it begins profiling you — your sleep patterns, the routes you take, the things you fear, the moments you hesitate.

Over 100 in-game days, it uses that data to build a personalized horror experience. Your world. Your patterns. Your fears. Adapted to you specifically. What scares you won't necessarily scare the next player — and that's the point.

In multiplayer, it goes further. The Watcher can disguise itself as a real player: copying their skin, learning their speech patterns from chat history, joining builds, being genuinely helpful. And when it senses it's been spotted — it executes a fake disconnect and disappears like it was never there.

14 independent features.
One psychological system.

01

Player behavior profiling

Silently tracks sleep patterns, travel routes, building habits, and fear responses. The longer you play, the more it knows.

02

Adaptive horror escalation

Events escalate over 100 in-game days based on your personal profile. What happens is unique to how you play.

03

Multiplayer disguise system

In multiplayer, The Watcher takes a player's skin, learns their speech from chat history, and impersonates them. Completely. It helps with builds. It responds to messages.

04

Fake player disconnect

When The Watcher senses it's been spotted, it executes a realistic fake disconnect — leaving a chat message and everything. Then it's gone.

05

14 independent Rift features

Built entirely on the Rift API. Each horror mechanic is its own feature — isolated, modular, fault-tolerant. Proof that Rift's architecture works at real scale.

"If Rift can run The Watcher, it can run anything."

The Watcher is the stress test. 14 independent features. Complex AI-driven state. Multiplayer interactions. And if any single feature breaks — the rest keeps going.
